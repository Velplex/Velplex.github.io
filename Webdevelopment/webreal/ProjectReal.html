Project One

This project was a school project where we were assigned a one word prompt and was told to build characters around that one word. A lot of students got very strange abstract words but the one word that was generated for me was the word robot, and so I drew and designed multiple robots with as different looks as I could to make them distinguishable from one another. Variations in body shape to accessories helped me build characters with recognizable silhouettes.

This was a very fun assignment to work on in class since I had such a simple and ride ranging prompt to work with. It was a tad difficult to finish much of it since we were under a strict deadline ‘cause the whole exercise was about coming up with ideas quickly but I’m still a fan of how it all turned out.

Project Two

This project was also a project that was assigned in class just like the previous project! The rules of this project was that the person next to us was told to give their neighbor a prompt and the person drawing had to create the character's personality and their visuals corresponding to the prompt their neighbor gave them. For this, my neighbor gave me the prompt of ‘a cartoon superhero team and an incompetent villain. The first picture is a line up of characters, the 2nd is the villain, and the third is an example of character explorations we had to do (this one being for the girl character)

This project was more difficult than the other two since I don’t watch much of action team cartoons like Voltron and I never have, but it was still a fun assignment since I got to work with my classmates on narrowing down each character and coming up with ideas.

Project Three

This project was an art request from a friend of mine who attends a nearby university. My friend asked of me to draw up a splash page and some location sketches from their book that they are writing that they wish to turn into a comic book and maybe even build an interactive website once they finished writing the complete story. Once I sent off the drawing they propositioned me to be the lead artist for their future comic book of the series and I was quick to agree!

I had the most fun with this project since I got to work with my friend about how to draw and color each character and what their outfits should be and it’s always an honor to work with people who are a fan of my silly cartoon style! I’m honored I was asked to work with her more in the future.
