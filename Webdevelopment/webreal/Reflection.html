Artists Statement + Inspirations

I am a self-taught artist that has been working and honing in on an art style for the past 12 years. I attend Mizzou and I am a part of the university's Digital storytelling major and I’m following the ‘Animation track.’ I’ve learned a lot about what to do and what not to do from Mizzou. Despite being well versed in drawing, learning Animation was a bit of struggle at the beginning but it was satisfying to finally get it down.

Much of my work has been inspired by the media that I have grown alongside and is mainly character design based. Outside of that I mainly create and wander from thought to thought in order to try and put something onto paper that's, hopefully, intriguing enough to garner a few seconds of a potential viewer's attention. I try to emphasize flowing lines in my drawings in order to make a sense of flow in my pieces that is hopefully satisfying for the viewer to look at.
My art tries to include a plethora of bright, eye-catching, colors that vary between being contested to colors that follow closely on the color wheel in order to give it a sense of uniformity. All of my work involves some kind of conversation between myself and what I'm aiming to achieve in each piece. Do teachers read these.. I hope not ‘cause I sound very pretentious right now. From my emotions, that could leak into my art to studies that I’ve conducted over the years to try and successfully replicate or define to a viewer.

My career goal since I was little was to land a position that included animation in some way since I’ve always loved animations for as long as I can remember. Character design has always drawn my eye the most since it's always been my favorite process when it comes to crafting a story. Other key interests are background design since it's one thing to create a character to give them personality and another to describe a character or an environment based on how it looks.
I’ve had a lot of inspiration throughout my years of learning to draw and design. Jakie Droujko and Mai Yoneyama have been two of the biggest inspirations to me when it comes to illustrations and design, but below are some more examples of inspirations and artists that my school has introduced to me.
